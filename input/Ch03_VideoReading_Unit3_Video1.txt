UNIT 3: BEAUTY AND APPEARANCE

VIDEO: The Future of Fashion p. 51
Narrator: It’s easy to look in a fashion magazine 
and want the latest expensive jeans, or the coolest 
sneakers. Globally, we are buying more and more in 
excessive amounts. Fast fashion, or cheaply produced 
clothing made to be worn for just a short time while 
it’s in fashion, constitutes a large piece of retail 
sales. People purchase it at an alarming rate. It takes 
resources to make clothing. Constantly buying new 
clothes is not good for the environment. So what can 
we do? We can turn to technology. 
Technology is everywhere and is now impacting our 
fashion choices, too. These clothes are for sale, but 
there is something distinctly different about them. 
They are being sold as a digital outfit. Social media 
users love to show off new outfits online, but a big 
drawback is that constantly buying new clothes is 
expensive and bad for the environment. So, fashion 
designer Regina Turbina is creating digital clothing at 
a much lower price. Perfect for an ever-changing yet 
still environmentally friendly wardrobe! Here, buyers 
can select the item they want and send a picture of 
themselves. Within 48 hours, individual pieces have 
been created that are tailored to the person. The 
buyer then receives a photo in the newly purchased 
outfit that they can post online. This gives the buyer 
a distinct new style or outfit, without actually getting 
more clothes. 
As the fashion industry evolves, it is offering more 
imaginative ways to design and shop for clothing. 
Buying digital clothing can help us avoid excessive 
purchasing, which helps the environment and our 
budgets, too.
